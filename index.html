FAQ

A Competency Demonstration Report (CDR) is required for all non-Australians seeking to work as engineers in Australia. A CDR report is a collection of documents that demonstrate your engineering skills as well as your English language proficiency. While writing the CDR report, you must ensure that your CDR follows all of the guidelines and specifications as per the Migration Skills Assessment booklet of Engineers Australia, the association responsible for hiring migrant engineers in Australia.

For an excellent CDR report, you should include the duration, job description of the duties, and team members who were involved in the problem resolution. Include technical details such as a schedule, task assignments to team members, and deadlines for each of the tasks. Also, if your CDR report contains technical creative design, it has a good chance of being approved by EA.

The CDR could be somewhere between 6000 and 8500 words long. However, a CDR should be no longer than 7000 words. Make certain that it addresses every aspect of your career episode. Each CE should be between 1,000 and 2,500 words in length. For each of your three career events, you must also create a one-page summary statement.

According to Engineers Australia, the assessment has no expiration date. Other requirements may be set by the Department of Home Affairs. Our belief is that they will consider letters up to three years old.
